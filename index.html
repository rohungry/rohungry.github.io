About

I lead the Analytics & AI group at Somatus, one of the first & largest digital specialty providers of kidney disease care in the world. My work sits at the intersection of AI, Data, Healthcare services, asking how we can we reduce the administrative burden of healthcare workers to reduce direct costs, improve quality, and increase patient satisfaction.

Previously, I was CTO at Lumiata, a predictive analytics platform company, where our work in AI and healthcare was featured in AAAI 2021 for best paper in the applied track. I was also a research scientist at Stanford School of Medicine, where I used statstical inference to identify biomarkers for lung cancer and COPD, and worked on clinical trials for Xolair in allergy immunotherapy. Prior to this, I worked at Transera, where I created a Bayesian contact center routing engine to optimze call conversions. I hold bachelors and masters degrees in pure math. I am also a big fan of music - I play five instruments, and spent a year and a half making lo-fi hip-hop beats, creating hundreds of tracks on soundcloud.

Interests

My work tackles a central question in AI for healthcare services:

How do we reduce the administrative burden on healthcare workers, so that they may spend more time with patients and less time on the computer?

I am also interested in several other problems, including voice AI in enterprise workflows, faster search enabling enterprise agentic workflows, methods for faster AI inference and training, autonomous cars and devices, and robotics in bench science.

Current projects include faster search and inference, and AI music generation inside Ableton Live.
